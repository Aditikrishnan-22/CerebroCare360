When did your symptoms start?
The speed of onset is one of the most critical clues in neurology. Be as accurate as possible.
CerebroBot Fact: Sudden onset (seconds–minutes) usually indicates a vascular emergency. Progressive onset over days–weeks suggests a mass lesion or inflammation. Episodic attacks point to migraine or epilepsy.

This tool does not diagnose brain tumors, stroke, migraine, epilepsy, dementia, or any other condition. It provides urgency guidance only, based on clinically described symptom patterns (Harrison's Principles of Internal Medicine). Cranial MRI with gadolinium contrast is the definitive test for suspected brain masses. Consult a qualified medical professional for diagnosis and treatment.
